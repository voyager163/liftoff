# Proposal: fix-seed-artifact-lifecycle

## Why

The scaffold's seeded OpenSpec change (`openspec/changes/bootstrap-<project>/`, four files) is recorded in the manifest like durable structure — but its own lifecycle ends in being archived (moved), which the reconciler and validator then fight: after archiving, `liftoff validate` fails with four "Missing artifact" errors and `liftoff update --apply` resurrects the archived change. Migrate's completion gate (`liftoff validate && liftoff doctor`) breaks the moment users follow the workflow it prescribes. Verified end-to-end on a 0.2.0 build (field report from the Orca migration, reproduced on a fresh scratch project).

## What Changes

- Generated artifacts split into two kinds: **owned** (structure Liftoff maintains — tracked in the manifest, restored/upgraded by update) and **seed** (starter content written once at create and then left to its own lifecycle — never tracked, never reconciled).
- The four bootstrap-change artifacts get a new `seed` category. They are still rendered and written at create time, but `buildManifest` excludes them from the recorded state.
- The reconcile engine skips `seed`-category render artifacts (otherwise they would classify as "new" forever once absent from the manifest).
- Legacy healing: the manifest loader drops known legacy seed entries (`openspec-seed-*`) on read, so projects generated by 0.2.0 heal immediately across `validate`, `update`, and `doctor`; the next `update --apply` persists the cleaned manifest.
- The emitted `migrate-to-liftoff` change already behaves this way (written outside the render); this change locks that behavior in with a regression test.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

None.

### Modified Capabilities

- `liftoff-project-scaffold`: The v2 manifest requirement narrows from "every generated artifact" to every **durable** generated artifact — seed content is written but not recorded.
- `liftoff-project-update`: New requirement — seed content is excluded from reconciliation and recorded state, including healing of legacy seed entries.

## Impact

- `src/templates.ts`: the four seed artifacts move to category `seed`; `buildManifest` filters that category.
- `src/file-system.ts`: `loadManifest` drops legacy `openspec-seed-*` entries on read.
- `src/reconcile.ts`: render artifacts with category `seed` are skipped.
- Tests: new seed-lifecycle coverage (archive seed → no drift, validate green; legacy manifest heals; `migrate-to-liftoff` archive stays invisible; seed files still written at create).
- Contract-safe: logicalName snapshot unchanged (seeds still render); manifest schema version stays 2 (entries simply stop appearing); regenerate the frozen manifest fixture so it stays representative.
- Release as 0.2.1 (patch).
